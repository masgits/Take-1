CULTURES

Kenya consists of almost 40 diverse cultures...

BAJUN

Bajuns are mostly found in the Coastal part of Kenya, Mombasa and Lamu. They use the 'Kibajuni' language which is a dialect of Bantu Swahili language. Most Bajuns are sailors or fishermen because their habitats are mostly located near oceans and water bodies.

ARAB

Arabs are among the minor ethnic group in Kenya with around 90% of them living in Mombasa Island. They mostly use the Swahili language because their ancestry is linked to their arab forefathers, however, many of them have been born, raised and lived in Mombasa and adapted to the Swahili culture.

SWAHILI

Swahili lifestyle is one of the best and peaceful lifestyle in the world. Once more, most Swahilis are located in Mombasa. Swahilis are often engaged in trading and selling of items such as mats, Kangas and other handmade crafts.

FOODS

If there are 40+ cultures and each culture has its own unique food, isn't Kenya the perfect place to be?

Mombasa is just the right place to treat yourself to delicious coastal foods such as pilau, mahamri which comes alongside pigeon peas with creamy coconut milk, chapati with beans or meat stew, fried cassava with chilli, fried sweet potatoes and what not.

Ugali is known as the staple food of Kenya which is made from cornmeal and hotwater and comes alongside any desired stew. Githeri is also another option which is a mixture of beans, potatoes, onions and sometimes meat, and Mutura which is also a mixture of green bananas, potatoes, tomatoes and some flavored spices.

Kenya has now developed by establishing restaurants and food courts which offer a taste of Western foods such as burgers, pizzas and Western salads. To name a few of the restaurants offering these foods, one could consider going to KFC, Subway, Snack Attack or a random Pizza Inn.

RELIGIONS

CHRISTIANITY
"The predominant religion in Kenya is Christianity, which is adhered to by an estimated 85.5% of the total population. Due to the proposed law of right to worship, there have been tremendous increase in number of churches in the country recently."

Source: Wikipedia

ISLAM
"Islam is the second largest religion in Kenya and the religion of 10.91 percent of the population. Muslims are concentrated mainly in the Coastal and North Eastern Regions. Nairobi has several mosques and a notable Muslim population. There are large and historically significant populations of Swahili Muslims on the coast (most notably in Mombasa, Lamu and Malindi), in the Western Province, and smaller numbers of Somali, Arab and South Asian Muslims."

Source: Wikipedia

HINDUISM
"There are Hindus living in Kenya. The numbers are estimated to be around 60,287 people or 0.13% of the population. They are mainly located in the capital of Kenya, Nairobi, and other urban areas such as Mombasa, Eldoret, Thika and Kisumu."

Source: Wikipedia
